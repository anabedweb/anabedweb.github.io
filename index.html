About Me

My name is Anabel Bedlan and I am a first-year Computer Science student at Western Washington University with a passion for all things computer science. As I continue my education, I’m eager to expand my knowledge with hands-on experience in the tech industry. I am particularly interested in software development, cybersecurity, and any other tech-related fields that allow me to grow my expertise and contribute to meaningful projects. At WWU, I am involved in multiple organizations, as I am a member of the Computer Science Distinguished Scholars program, as well as an active member of the Associated Computing Machinery Club and the Competitive Programming Club. Three these activities I have been able to collaborate with peers and improve upon my technical skills. Outside of school, I have gained incredibly valuable experience through my time working as a programmer on Pierce College science Dome’s 2023 NASA Space Grant project, along with working as a system programmer and ambassador for the Dome. Through these roles, I gained the ability to quickly learn new programming languages, work under tight deadlines, and collaborate with a team to achieve project goals. Outside of both school and work experience, I enjoy participating in hackathons, Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges, and working on personal projects in my free time. I have significant experience with Java, C#, C++, JavaScript, Python, and HTML, and have worked with R, Go, and Swift. I also have experience in video game development, web development, app development, and various cybersecurity skills. I am actively seeking internship opportunities where I can apply my skills, learn from experienced professionals, and contribute to real-world projects. My goal is to gain practical experience, deepen my technical knowledge, and make meaningful contributions to the tech community.
